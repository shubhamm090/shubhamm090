About This Website

Know Your Herb is an informative platform created to help users explore and understand the rich diversity of Indian medicinal herbs. This website features 20 widely known herbal plants, including their local names, scientific classifications, locations they are found in, and traditional health benefits.

India has a long-standing history of herbal medicine, especially through the Ayurvedic system. Many of these herbs are still used today in home remedies and traditional healing. With this website, we aim to promote awareness, conservation, and proper usage of these natural resources.

Whether you're a student, researcher, nature enthusiast, or someone simply curious about herbal healing, Know Your Herb is your digital companion to dive into the green world of herbal knowledge.

Tulsi

scientific Name: Ocimum sanctum

Common Names: Holy Basil, Sacred Basil

Habitat: Native to India, commonly found in gardens.

Medicinal Uses: Known for its anti-inflammatory, anti-viral, and antioxidant properties.

Cultivation: Prefers warm climates and well-drained soil. Grows well in pots or garden beds.

Neem

Scientific Name: Azadirachta indica

Common Names: Margosa, Indian Lilac

Habitat: Found in tropical and semi-tropical regions of India.

Medicinal Uses: Used for skin disorders, blood purification, and insect repellent.

Cultivation: Requires full sunlight and tolerates dry conditions.

Ashwagandha

Scientific Name: Withania somnifera

Common Names: Indian Ginseng, Winter Cherry

Habitat: Common in dry regions of India.

Medicinal Uses: Reduces stress and anxiety, boosts immunity.

Cultivation: Grows in dry soil with full sun exposure.

Amla

Scientific Name: Phyllanthus emblica

Common Names: Indian Gooseberry

Habitat: Found in deciduous forests across India.

Medicinal Uses: Boosts immunity, improves digestion, rich in vitamin C.

Cultivation: Prefers well-drained loamy soil and tropical climates.

Giloy

Scientific Name: Tinospora cordifolia

Common Names: Guduchi, Amrita

Habitat: Found in tropical regions, climbing on trees.

Medicinal Uses: Enhances immunity, reduces fever, anti-inflammatory.

Cultivation: Climbs on trees, grows in fertile and moist soil.

Turmeric

Scientific Name: Curcuma longa

Common Names: Haldi

Habitat: Commonly grown in South Asia.

Medicinal Uses: Anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antioxidant properties.

Cultivation: Needs warm temperatures and well-drained soil.

Common Names: Licorice

Medicinal Uses: Soothes sore throat, boosts immunity, relieves indigestion.

Cultivation: Prefers light, sandy soil with good drainage.
